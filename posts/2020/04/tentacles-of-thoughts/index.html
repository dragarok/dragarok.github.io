Tentacles of Thoughts

Inside the dark vents of my body,

Lies the vines: of growing darkness.

Trying to find it’s way to my brain,

It sweeps, explodes

It grunges from the inside.

There used to be garden inside,

Thoughts laid peace within me.

Fights were always physical then,

Peace was a habit,

And thoughts resembled curiosity then.

The darkness grew out of the worldly ways,

The stories of success,

The stories of failures,

The horrors of living,

The ephemeral life,

And the drive to thrive.

Now,

With a gasp it starts,

Slowly it finds it’s way

Creepy they are,

Creeping through me like swinging vines,

Stranded and clogged.

Walls of mind and tentacles of thoughts,

The war of the microcosm,

The dance of the devil form.

My bones are still the same,

My muscles undeformed,

But it feels like

The quarks inside them want some deadly transform.

Cold it is,

To fight with ownself.

Hard it is,

To break the chains of thoughts.

Pain it is,

To not understand anyone but ownself.

Peace it is,

To lay inside the box in peace

With tentacles of thoughts buried to the ground.
