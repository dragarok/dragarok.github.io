Brain Facts

Brain Physiology:

Representation

ID: 85b03664-e44a-4ba2-96f8-636cdd41b7c8

Description

  1. Cerebrum
    • Largest part of brain
  2. Corpus Callosum
    • Pathway to connect left and right hemisphere of brain
  3. Cerebral Cortex
    • Folded structure of cerebrum
  4. Frontal Lobe
    • Part of cerebral cortex just above the eyes
    • Voluntary Movement, Planning, High level Cognitive Skills such as problem solving
  5. Parietal Lobe
    • Region just behind the frontal lobe
    • Responsible for sensory processing and some visual processing
  6. Occipital Lobe
    • Part of cerebral cortex at the back of the head
    • Visual processing: Recognizing color and shapes and understanding visual input
  7. Temporal Lobe
    • Part of C.C. at the sides of the head
    • Responsible for visual processing and interpreting auditory information.
  8. Hippocampus
    • Curved structure below cerebral cortex
    • Responsible for forming memories
  9. Amygdala
    • Responsible for integrating emotions and memories
  10. Limbic System
    • Hippocampus and amygdala together
    • Responsible for regulating emotion and motivation
  11. Thalamus
    • Sensory information and relay it to other parts of the brain
  12. Forebrain
    • All these structures with cerebral cortex
  13. Midbrain
    • Beneath the thalamus
    • Coordinate eye movement such as blinking, focusing , triggering reflexes based on loud noise
    • Fine motor control skills such as writing with pen or playing a musical instrument
  14. Hindbrain
    • Glucose regulation, sleep and help control movement

  15. Basal Ganglia

  16. Cerebellum
    • Tucked underneath occipital lobe
    • Coordinate movement and learn new motor skills
  17. Pons
    • Breathing and posture control
  18. Medulla
    • Nerve pathways from brain to spinal cord

  19. Brainstem

  20. Thalamocortical loops and EEG
  21. Brain Waves Frequencies
