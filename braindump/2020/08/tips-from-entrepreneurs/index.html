Tips from entrepreneurs

Being an entrepreneur old fashioned way

I Became an Entrepreneur the Old-Fashioned Way: Getting Fired | by Todd Brison

  • Companies are designed to make you feel rich, powerful, and successful. They are also designed to keep you from remembering all of that wealth, power, and status can be ripped away at a moment’s notice.
  • Getting fired can take people to pursue their side gig.

Tips

  • Work harder on streams of income

    • Work harder on streams of income where money flows in. If you have to spend time on secondary source of income, you have two jobs.
    • No amount of hustle beats strategy when it comes to earning more money.

  • Fight for yourself at work

    • Don’t get romantic about work. Titles matter. Power matters. Be nice but don’t be naive.

  • Build a better network

    • In corporate world, if nobody sees you, you are just an invisible clog in a big machine.
    • Don’t overwork yourself for a company that doesn’t care. Use extra hours connecting to people who love what you do and tell others about you.

  • Tell people how you feel

    • Sometimes people are too wrong about conclusions. They are wrong about how they treat people. Say what needs to be said. People in trenches will respect you for standing up for them. People in power will respect your backbone.

  • Learn how business works

    • When working, most things happen by magic. Big companies allow you to ignore all the moving pieces and focus on your corner of the universe. Lean the little things while you have a safety net.

  • Be competitive

    • At a job, you can be competitive or not. You’ll still get paid. Entrepreneurship is you against the world. If you aren’t competitive, probably turn around and go back to sea of worker bees.

  • Last but not the least: Don’t get too comfortable.

    • Do everything you can to your limits but your glorious day is yet far away. So, keep building. You can never do too much.
